Thermal EverRock Insulated Skirting: The Cold-Climate Choice in the Everlock Vinyl Line

Most mobile home skirting does one job: close the gap between the home and the ground. Thermal EverRock does two. The foam-backed insulating layer reduces cold air infiltration under the home, which matters in states where pipes can freeze or where heating bills run high. The stone-texture surface means it also looks better than basic vinyl, giving your home a refined appearance without the higher cost of true composite stone.

Thermal EverRock combines the aesthetic appeal and durability of our popular EverRock Vinyl with an insulating panel construction. This means you get the best of both: the visual upgrade of a stone-texture finish with the energy and pipe-protection benefits of insulation. Manufactured by Everlock Systems and sold factory-direct.

If you live in a cold climate but don't need the maximum insulation performance of our Gorilla Insulated Skirting, Thermal EverRock is the more accessible insulated option. It holds temperatures better than non-insulated vinyl, protects pipes in moderate cold, and reduces heat loss through the perimeter.

What Makes Thermal EverRock Different

The key difference is construction. Non-insulated vinyl is a single panel layer. Thermal EverRock uses foam-backed panel construction, which adds an insulating layer between the vinyl surface and the gap underneath the home.

This foam layer reduces cold air infiltration. In winter, cold air naturally tries to move upward and into the home through gaps and seams. The insulating barrier slows this transfer, which means:

  • Pipes under the home are less likely to freeze in moderate cold
  • The heating system works less hard to maintain floor temperatures
  • Energy efficiency improves, particularly in homes with exposed water lines near the perimeter

The surface still uses the same stone-texture profile as EverRock Vinyl. From the outside, it looks identical to our non-insulated stone-texture product. The difference is in the panel construction that you never see.

Think of it this way: if EverRock Vinyl is the stone-texture look for mild climates, Thermal EverRock is the same look with thermal protection built in. It's one product, one aesthetic, two performance levels.

Who Needs Insulated Skirting

Insulated skirting makes sense if you fit one or more of these categories:

  • Cold climates: Homes in the Midwest, Mountain West, New England, or northern tier states where winter temperatures regularly drop below freezing
  • Exposed water lines: Homes with water supply lines or drain lines exposed near the perimeter where freezing is a risk
  • Heating bills: Homes where you notice heating costs are higher than expected, suggesting cold air infiltration under the floor
  • FHA/VA appraisals: Jurisdictions where insulated skirting is encouraged or preferred by lenders. Check with your appraiser
  • New installations: If you're replacing skirting in a cold climate and want to solve freezing problems once and for all

If you're in a mild climate where freezing is rare or never happens, non-insulated EverRock vinyl will serve you just as well at a lower price point.

Cold Climate Map: Where Insulated Skirting Makes Sense

Thermal EverRock Insulated is ideal for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 6, which covers most of the northern half of the country. This includes:

  • Mountain West: High-elevation communities in Colorado, Wyoming, Montana, and Utah where winter temperatures regularly drop to minus 10 to minus 20 degrees Fahrenheit
  • Midwest: Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, Illinois, and Iowa, where sub-zero winters are common
  • Northeast: Upstate New York, New England, and Mid-Atlantic states with sustained freezing and heavy snow
  • Northern tier: North Dakota, South Dakota, and northern Nebraska with extended cold seasons
  • High-altitude zones: Anywhere above 6,000 feet elevation where pipes freeze easily

If you're unsure whether your region qualifies, call 800-843-3336 and describe your location. We can advise whether Thermal EverRock or non-insulated vinyl is the better fit for your specific climate.

Installation timeline: Most DIYers complete a single-wide installation in a weekend. Allow longer than you would for non-insulated vinyl because of panel weight.

Complete kits: Available with all panels, hardware, and access doors included. This removes guesswork about part counts and panel sequences.

Thermal EverRock Insulated Skirting: Common Questions

How much insulation does Thermal EverRock actually provide? +

Thermal EverRock reduces cold air infiltration under the home compared to non-insulated vinyl. It is not a substitute for proper pipe insulation in extreme climates (like Alaska), but it makes a meaningful difference in moderate cold-climate conditions. It slows heat loss and protects pipes in freezing temperatures typical of the northern half of the country.

Is it harder to install than regular EverRock? +

The panel is heavier, so installation takes longer and you may want an extra person to help. The rail system is the same. Most DIYers complete a single-wide in a weekend, but allow more time than you would for non-insulated vinyl. If you prefer professional installation, most contractors handle insulated panels routinely.

How does Thermal EverRock compare to Gorilla Insulated? +

Both are insulated vinyl products. Gorilla is heavier-duty and costs slightly more ($3,101 vs $2,974). For most cold-climate homes, Thermal EverRock is adequate. Gorilla is the step up for extreme climates or maximum insulation performance.

Can I get a sample before ordering? +

Yes. Order samples at samples.html. We provide samples of all our vinyl products so you can see the stone-texture finish and feel the panel construction.

Does the insulation affect the appearance? +

No. The stone-texture surface looks identical to EverRock Vinyl from the outside. The insulation is in the panel construction, which you never see. The aesthetic is the same.
