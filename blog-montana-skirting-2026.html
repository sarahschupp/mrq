Why Montana Manufactured Home Owners Need Durable Skirting

Montana has one of the largest manufactured housing populations west of the Mississippi. It also has one of the most demanding climates for skirting. Temperatures regularly drop to minus 30 degrees Fahrenheit in Cut Bank, Havre, and Glasgow. The eastern plains see sustained winds of 40 to 50 miles per hour for weeks at a time. Many Montana homeowners live 30 miles or more from the nearest contractor, which means getting a skirting problem fixed quickly is not always possible.

In Montana, skirting choice is not about convenience or appearance alone. It is about survival and property protection. The right skirting system keeps pipes from freezing, prevents wind damage, and holds up under conditions that would destroy inferior products in a single season. Montana's Two Skirting Challenges: Cold and Wind

Extreme Cold

Montana winters are severe. Cut Bank averages 79 days below zero Fahrenheit annually. Havre and Glasgow see similar conditions. These are not brief cold snaps. They are sustained periods of extreme cold that test every building system in a manufactured home.

Skirting material matters less in extreme cold than pipe protection does. Even insulated skirting cannot prevent freezing if your backup systems fail. Skirting is one part of a complete freeze-prevention strategy that also includes heat tape on the most vulnerable pipes, proper ventilation closure in winter, and heating of the foundation space if necessary.

High Wind Loads

The eastern Montana plains regularly experience sustained winds of 40 to 50 miles per hour. Mountain passes between the Rockies and the plains funnel wind with even greater intensity. In some places, these are not occasional storms but normal winter weather.

Wind puts massive pressure on skirting panels. Lightweight vinyl panels flex outward, create gaps, rattle against the frame, and can separate entirely. Heavier insulated panels resist flex and stay locked to the rail system. The Everlock triple-lock rail design prevents panels from lifting or separating under sustained pressure.

Both challenges point to the same answer: heavier, insulated skirting that locks tightly to the home frame and resists both temperature swings and wind pressure.

Wind Resistance: How Skirting Handles Montana's Pressure

Wind resistance depends on three factors: panel weight, panel material, and rail design.

Panel Weight: Heavier panels have lower surface area per unit mass, which reduces wind flex. Gorilla Insulated panels are the heaviest in our lineup. They resist the outward pressure that causes panels to separate from rails.

Panel Material: Rigid composite materials like those in Novik and insulated panels resist wind better than flexible vinyl. Standard vinyl can flex significantly under sustained 40+ mph wind, creating gaps and noise. Insulated panels, with their core structure, maintain rigidity.

Rail Design: The Everlock triple-lock rail system has three locking points that grip the panel from top, side, and bottom. This design prevents the outward lift that breaks weaker rail systems under wind load.

Installation is Critical: Proper installation of the bottom rail into solid ground is as important as product choice. Panels anchored correctly at top and bottom move as a system rather than individually. The bottom rail must be fully seated and secured with anchors every 16 inches. Professional installation is recommended for high-wind areas.

Pipe Freeze Prevention in Montana

Insulated skirting is important for freeze protection, but it is not sufficient on its own. At minus 30 degrees Fahrenheit, even R-13 insulation needs backup measures.

The complete freeze-prevention system:

  • Insulated skirting (Thermal EverRock or Gorilla) that reduces heat loss through the perimeter
  • Heat tape on all exposed plumbing, especially outdoor spigots and plumbing runs along exterior walls
  • Closed foundation vents in winter to trap interior heat in the foundation space
  • Optional: a thermostat-controlled under-home heater for extreme exposure conditions

Skirting alone will not keep pipes from freezing in minus 30-degree weather. The combination of insulated skirting plus heat tape plus vent management works. Talk to us before winter about your specific setup.

In rural Montana, it's better to overinvest in freeze prevention than to face a burst pipe 30 miles from the nearest plumber.

DIY vs Professional Installation in Rural Montana

Montana homeowners have a strong tradition of doing their own work. Everlock Deluxe and EverRock complete kits are designed for DIY installation.

DIY Installation Works Well If:

  • Your home is accessible and ground conditions are not extreme
  • You have a helper or two for heavy panels
  • You are comfortable following detailed instructions and working with basic tools
  • You have ordered the complete kit with all parts in one shipment

Consider Professional Installation If:

  • Your home sits on a slope or has challenging foundation access
  • You are installing Gorilla Insulated panels, which are heavier than vinyl
  • Your location is extremely wind-prone and you want engineered installation for maximum hold
  • You need the work done quickly and you cannot spare the time

The Montana advantage of complete kits: When you order one of our complete kits, everything arrives at once, materials and hardware. You do not have to worry about missing parts or ordering twice. This matters in rural Montana, where a second freight run can add weeks to the timeline.

Montana Mobile Home Skirting: Common Questions

What is the best skirting for a home on the eastern Montana plains? +

Gorilla Insulated. The combination of highest insulation (R-13) and the heaviest panel weight holds up better in sustained high wind than any other product we offer. If budget is a concern, Thermal EverRock is the next best choice.

Can panels blow off in a Montana wind event? +

Properly installed panels anchored top and bottom should not separate or blow off. The Everlock rail system is designed to keep panels locked under wind pressure. Installation quality matters as much as product choice. Ensure the bottom rail is fully seated and anchored every 16 inches.

Do you ship to rural Montana addresses? +

Yes, we ship throughout the contiguous United States, including remote Montana addresses. Standard freight carriers serve most communities. For very remote locations, call 800-843-3336 before ordering so we can confirm delivery options and timelines.

Is Thermal EverRock enough for a Montana winter, or do I need Gorilla? +

For Billings, Missoula, or Helena with moderate-to-good home placement, Thermal EverRock handles most winters well. For the eastern plains, high-elevation locations, or extreme wind exposure, Gorilla Insulated is the safer choice. Call us with your specific location and we can advise.

Can I install Gorilla Insulated skirting myself? +

Yes, but the panels are heavier than standard vinyl. Two people makes the job much easier. Our installation guide includes video walkthroughs. Call us with fit questions before you order so we can help you avoid any surprises during installation.
