The "Everlock EZ Storage" link appears in the site navigation and is reachable from the homepage, but the destination page returns a 404. Every visitor who clicks this nav item hits a dead end. This is an active navigation error.
ⓘ
No sitemap.xml found
The site does not have a sitemap.xml at any standard location (/sitemap.xml, /sitemap_index.xml, /sitemap/). Search engines rely on sitemaps for efficient crawling, especially for e-commerce sites. This should be generated and submitted to Google Search Console.
ⓘ
Zero meta descriptions across all 27 pages
Not a single page has a meta description tag. Google will auto-generate snippets, but those are often poor quality. Well-written meta descriptions directly impact click-through rate from search results pages and are a fundamental SEO gap.
ⓘ
Page title format is inconsistent across sections
Product pages use at least three different title patterns: (1) "[Product] Skirting from SkirtingDirect.com", (2) "View [Product] at Skirting Direct — Your Factory Direct Source...", and (3) raw Merchant5 titles like "EverBrick II, EverStone II, Premium EverStone II, River Rock & Field Stone Calculator: :". Two support pages have mis-matched titles: Installation Instructions shows "Privacy Policy" and Customer Gallery shows "About Us".
ⓘ
Duplicate nav entries: "Gorilla Insulated" and "Thermal EverRock" both link to /everlock/gorilla.html
The mobile/collapsed nav menu lists "Gorrilla Thermal" and "Thermal EverRock" as separate items, but both resolve to the same page. This likely reflects a product line consolidation that was not cleaned up in the nav template. Also note the typo: "Gorrilla" in the collapsed nav.
ⓘ
Technical: Merchant5 URLs expose platform internals
Calculator and e-commerce pages live under /Merchant5/ with URLs like /Merchant5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PLST. These expose the underlying Miva Merchant platform and are not SEO-friendly. The "All Products", "Terms & Conditions", and "Warranty" pages are only accessible via these parameter-based URLs, which are difficult for search engines to index reliably.

Overview & Site Hierarchy
SkirtingDirect.com is a small, flat DTC e-commerce site. All product pages are exactly 1 click from the homepage. The site has a simple structure that is easy to navigate but has limited SEO depth.

About the Site

Skirting Direct is the consumer-facing website of Everlock Systems, Inc., a Boulder, Colorado company with nearly 40 years in the mobile/manufactured home skirting industry. The site was created in 2011 to give homeowners direct access to factory pricing on skirting products that are typically sold through dealers.

The site sells a focused product line: traditional vinyl skirting (Everlock Deluxe, Gorilla Insulated), rock-look vinyl skirting (EverRock, Thermal EverRock), and premium Novik-branded stone/brick skirting panels. Accessories include foundation vents, floor repair kits, and access doors. Each product family has a dedicated landing page and a matching product calculator.

The platform is Miva Merchant (evidenced by /Merchant5/ paths). The site is visually straightforward, using a Bootstrap-based responsive layout with a horizontal nav dropdown.

Strategic Observations
High-level findings and recommendations for content strategy, GEO, and site growth.

What the Site Does Well

The site is clean, focused, and navigable. Every product is one click from the homepage, product pages include color selectors and matching calculators, and the FAQ covers the highest-friction purchase questions (shipping costs, freight delivery, LTL vs residential). The 40-year company history and factory-direct positioning are genuinely differentiating claims.

The calculator tools are a meaningful conversion asset — helping customers calculate material needs before ordering is a direct way to reduce returns and support tickets.

Site Thinness and SEO Vulnerability

With 27 total pages (13 product/company pages of real content, the rest utility), this site has almost no topical authority for informational queries. A homeowner searching "how to install mobile home skirting" or "best skirting for manufactured home" will not find this site in organic results — there is no editorial content at all.

This is the single largest SEO gap. Competitors or content aggregators that produce how-to guides, comparison articles, and buyer's guides will consistently outrank SkirtingDirect.com for the high-volume informational queries that drive purchase consideration.

GEO / AI Visibility Gap

For AI-generated answers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini), the site currently has very limited signals. There is no structured data (Product schema would allow price, availability, and reviews to appear in rich results), no FAQ schema (the FAQ page content is ideal for this), and no llms.txt file to guide AI crawlers.

The "Everlock Systems" and "Skirting Direct" brand names are not well-established in AI training data relative to their 40-year history. A targeted GEO strategy — expert-attributed content, structured data, and an llms.txt — would meaningfully improve brand citation in AI-generated responses about mobile home skirting.

Content Architecture Opportunity

A hub-and-spoke content model built around the core topic "mobile home skirting" would address both the SEO and GEO gaps simultaneously. A central hub page ("Complete Guide to Mobile Home Skirting") with spokes covering installation, cost comparison, material types, color selection, and maintenance would establish topical authority and provide the citation-worthy content that AI systems prefer.

The existing FAQ content is a natural starting point — it contains genuine customer questions that could be expanded into standalone articles. The installation instructions page already links to video content, which could anchor a richer resource section.

Quick Win Priorities

Fix immediately (no content effort): The /everlock/storage.html 404, the two mis-titled support pages (Installation Instructions and Customer Gallery), and the "Mobil" typo. These are template-level errors that can be corrected in the Miva Merchant CMS.

Fix in under a week: Add meta descriptions to all product and company pages. Generate and submit a sitemap.xml to Google Search Console. Remove the duplicate "Gorilla Insulated" nav entry.

Medium-term (content): Write meta descriptions, standardize title tag format, add Product and FAQ schema markup. Consider one well-written buyer's guide as an initial content investment.
