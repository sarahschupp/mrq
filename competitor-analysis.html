Top-Level Summary
Biggest threat
SkirtingDepot.com
Sells the exact same Novik products in complete pre-packaged kits by home size, with transparent pricing and phone support. Removes all friction from the buying decision that SkirtingDirect currently leaves on the table.
Best content model
DuraSkirt.com
Different material (concrete), but the strongest content strategy in the category: comparison pages, installation videos, engineering submittals, testimonials, FAQs, blog, and contractor channel. The content architecture is worth studying even though the product doesn't compete directly.
Best UX model
Trex.com
Not a competitor, but the gold standard for how a building materials brand helps customers buy: Color Selector, Deck Designer, Cost Calculator, Comparison Tool, sample ordering, and a Pro program. These tools exist because they directly close sales. SkirtingDirect's calculators are a start — but Trex shows what full tool coverage looks like.

Key Learnings for SkirtingDirect
✓Comparison content works. DuraSkirt's "vs Home Depot" and "vs Lowes" pages directly capture high-intent shoppers who are price-comparing. SkirtingDirect has a real advantage over box-store vinyl (quality, selection, direct pricing) but no content that makes that case.

△Product focus matters. DuraSkirt has expanded into wheel stops, garden beds, and skincare (!) — some mission drift that dilutes brand authority. SkirtingDirect should stay focused on the skirting category.

Key Learnings for SkirtingDirect
⚠The package model is a direct challenge. A customer who knows their home size (14x70, 16x80, etc.) can land on SkirtingDepot, pick their package, and check out in three clicks. On SkirtingDirect, they need to understand what panels, rails, and hardware they need separately. SkirtingDirect has the calculator advantage — but the package model removes the need for a calculator entirely.
✓Where SkirtingDirect wins: product selection (Novik stone + Gorilla insulated + Thermal EverRock + accessories), content depth (FAQs, installation instructions), and the calculator tools. SkirtingDepot has none of that.
△Consider a "Complete Kit" product line. SkirtingDirect could offer the same package-first model on top of its existing catalog. A "14x70 Complete Everlock Deluxe Kit" would directly compete with SkirtingDepot's most popular SKUs while keeping the brand's factory-direct story intact.
△Pricing context matters. SkirtingDepot shows prices on all listing pages, making comparison easy. SkirtingDirect's product pages don't show aggregated package prices, only per-panel or per-piece pricing that requires calculator use to understand total cost.

Key Learnings for SkirtingDirect
✓A "visualizer" is the single highest-value tool for Novik products. A customer choosing between Stacked Stone in Sandstone vs Weathered Gray for their home's foundation cannot make that decision from a 100px color chip. A photo-upload visualizer (like ColorView) or even a simulated home rendering would directly increase conversion on the premium Novik line.
✓ColorCoach's guided approach is the right model for a less technical buyer. CertainTeed's ColorCoach walks users through style preferences and home characteristics before suggesting colors — it's a guided selling tool. △Don't copy the enterprise complexity. CertainTeed's heavy JavaScript and multi-layer navigation would be overkill and an SEO liability at SkirtingDirect's scale. Extract the visual tool concept; leave the enterprise architecture.
Strategic Opportunities
Priority-ranked opportunities identified from competitive research. Ordered by impact and feasibility.
📦
Complete Package SKUs
Beat SkirtingDepot at their own game
High Priority
SkirtingDepot's core product is a "Complete [home size] Skirting Package" — everything the customer needs in one SKU at one price. SkirtingDirect currently sells components, which requires customers to use the calculator, add multiple items to cart, and trust they've gotten it right. Adding a "Complete Kit" product layer on top of the existing component catalog would directly attack SkirtingDepot's primary advantage. You could offer these for the top 5-8 most common home sizes (14x70, 16x80, etc.) across the Everlock Deluxe, EverRock, and Gorilla product lines.
📝
Meta Descriptions + Title Fixes
Zero-effort SEO recovery
High Priority
Every competitor has meta descriptions; SkirtingDirect has none on any of its 27 pages. This is a single-session fix that could meaningfully improve search CTR. The pattern from MobileHomePartsDepot is instructive: lead with "complete [product] package", include home size language ("single wide, double wide"), and close with a shipping or price promise. Also fix the two mis-titled pages (Installation Instructions showing "Privacy Policy"; Customer Gallery showing "About Us") and the broken navigation link to /everlock/storage.html.
🏆
Comparison Content Hub
Model after DuraSkirt's "vs" pages
High Priority
DuraSkirt captures high-intent search traffic with "DURASKIRT vs Home Depot Mobile Home Skirting" and "vs Lowes" pages. SkirtingDirect has the same opportunity — and arguably a stronger story. A page comparing Everlock Deluxe to box-store vinyl skirting, one comparing EverRock to K-Rok, and one comparing vinyl vs Novik stone would all target mid-funnel comparison queries. These pages also serve GEO — they're exactly the kind of specific, structured comparison content that AI systems cite when answering "which mobile home skirting is best?"
🎥
Installation Video Library
DuraSkirt's #1 content asset
Medium Priority
DuraSkirt has 9+ specific installation video topics covering every anxiety a DIY buyer has: backfilling, joint concealing, crawl space access, dryer vent holes, etc. SkirtingDirect has one installation page with a couple of external YouTube links. A structured video library — even 5-6 videos covering the key Everlock Deluxe and EverRock installation scenarios — would reduce support calls, increase buyer confidence, and generate organic video search traffic. The installation page URL (/page/cp-installation-instructions.html) is also poorly structured; it should be at /installation/ or /how-to-install/.
🌞
Visual Color Tool for Novik
Model after CertainTeed's ColorView
Medium Priority
The Novik premium stone products (5 styles, 6-8 colors each) are a visual purchase. A customer cannot choose between Weathered Gray and Sandstone from a color swatch. A visualizer — even a simplified one showing each color option applied to a representative foundation section — would directly help the premium product line convert. Full photo-upload tools (like CertainTeed's ColorView) are expensive to build; a curated photo gallery organized by color and style is a simpler first step that provides the same functional value.
📷
Sample Ordering
Both Trex and DuraSkirt offer this
Medium Priority
Both Trex and DuraSkirt offer physical samples. For a $400-2,800+ purchase decision, holding the material removes the biggest remaining hesitation. DuraSkirt charges a nominal fee for samples, which is standard practice. SkirtingDirect doesn't mention samples anywhere on the site. Even a "request a sample" form or a $5 sample product in the Merchant5 store would address this gap — and the sample itself is a direct marketing piece that lives in the customer's hands until they're ready to order.
📋
FHA/VA Compliance Documentation
DuraSkirt uses this as a trust differentiator
Medium Priority
A significant portion of manufactured home buyers are financing with FHA or VA loans, which require that skirting meet specific inspection standards. DuraSkirt publishes engineering submittals and compliance documentation directly on its site and positions this as a buying reason ("Qualifies for VA, FHA, and all other loan requirements"). If Everlock products meet these requirements (very likely given 40 years in the business), a single page documenting this compliance — and ideally a downloadable PDF — would close a meaningful segment of buyers who are blocked by financing requirements.
💸
Cost Calculator Upgrade
From "how many panels" to "total cost estimate"
Medium Priority
SkirtingDirect already has 5 product calculators — a genuine advantage over SkirtingDepot and DuraSkirt. The current calculators output a parts list. Upgrading them to output a total estimated cost (panels + rails + hardware) would make them conversion devices, not just utility tools. Trex's Deck Cost Calculator is the model: the user enters dimensions and gets a dollar amount, which then links directly to an add-to-cart flow. This upgrade may require Miva Merchant customization but the payoff on conversion rate would be significant.
