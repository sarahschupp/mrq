EverRock Vinyl: The Mid-Range Vinyl Option

EverRock Vinyl splits the difference between traditional flat vinyl and cast-polymer faux stone. The panel surface has a stone-like textured profile that adds visual dimension and a more finished appearance, while remaining lighter and easier to install than the Novik product line.

If Everlock Deluxe Vinyl gives you a clean, traditional profile and the Novik line gives you cast-polymer stone replication, EverRock sits in between. It's the choice for homeowners who want more character in the skirting appearance without jumping to the Novik price range.

What EverRock Vinyl Looks Like

EverRock Vinyl features a stone-texture surface profile on a standard vinyl panel. The horizontal ridges and surface contours create depth and visual interest that reads like stone from a distance, while the vinyl material remains lightweight and easy to handle.

Unlike flat Everlock Deluxe Vinyl, EverRock has dimensional texture. Unlike cast-polymer Novik panels, EverRock is vinyl, so it won't have the depth and realism of true faux stone, but it offers significantly more character than a plain panel.

EverRock is available in multiple colors that complement most home exteriors and satisfy HOA appearance requirements. If you need to match a specific exterior color or community aesthetic, call 800-843-3336 and our team can help you choose.

EverRock vs Everlock Deluxe Vinyl

Key distinction: visual texture. Deluxe Vinyl has a clean, flat traditional panel profile. EverRock has a stone-like textured surface that adds dimension and character.

Both use the same DIY-friendly panel-and-rail installation method. Both are UV-stabilized vinyl that hold up well to sun exposure. Deluxe Vinyl is the entry-level option for budget-conscious homeowners who want solid quality at the lowest cost. EverRock is for those who want more visual appeal and don't mind spending a moderate premium.

Choose Deluxe Vinyl if: Budget is the priority, or the home will be covered with skirting that won't be visible from the street.

Choose EverRock if: You want the skirting to be a visual feature of the home, or you're in an HOA community with appearance standards.

EverRock vs Novik Faux Stone

Core difference: material and realism. EverRock is a vinyl panel with a textured surface. Novik is cast polymer that replicates actual stone or brick, with considerably more depth and realism.

EverRock is lighter, easier to install, and significantly less expensive. Novik products start at from $3,320 for the reference home. Novik offers five distinct products: Hand-Laid Brick, Stacked Stone, Premium Hand-Cut Stone, Field Stone, and Dry Stacked Stone. Each has a unique appearance that more closely mimics the real material.

Choose EverRock if: You want moderate visual texture at a mid-range price, and installation ease matters.

Choose Novik if: You need the depth and realism of actual stone or brick appearance, FHA/VA appraisers prefer heavier materials, or you're in a high-end community with strict appearance standards.

EverRock vs Thermal EverRock Insulated

Same texture profile, different insulation layer. Thermal EverRock uses the same stone-texture surface with an added insulating layer. Pricing for Thermal EverRock starts at from $2,974 for the reference home.

Choose standard EverRock Vinyl if: Your climate is moderate, pipes don't run under the home, or you're in a warm region like Florida or the Southwest.

Choose Thermal EverRock if: You need energy efficiency and climate control for pipes running under the home, or you're in a cold or variable climate where winter temperatures drop below freezing for extended periods.

FHA and VA Loans

EverRock Vinyl satisfies HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. The stone-texture surface gives appraisers confidence in durability, and the material is recognized as a compliant skirting option for both FHA and VA loan products.

However, final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. Some lenders have specific preferences about material type or appearance. Always verify your lender's requirements in writing before ordering.

If you're applying for an FHA or VA loan, contact your lender first to confirm EverRock meets their standards, then call us at 800-843-3336 for a quote.

EverRock Vinyl: Common Questions

What is the difference between EverRock and Everlock Deluxe Vinyl? +

EverRock has a stone-texture surface profile that adds visual dimension. Deluxe Vinyl has a clean, flat traditional panel profile. Both use the same DIY installation method and are UV-stabilized vinyl. The choice comes down to whether you want the extra visual appeal of the textured surface.

Is there an insulated version of EverRock? +

Yes. Thermal EverRock Insulated uses the same stone-texture profile with an added insulation layer underneath. It starts at from $2,974 for the reference home. Choose Thermal EverRock if pipes run under your home or you're in a cold climate.

Can I install EverRock myself? +

Yes. EverRock uses the same DIY-friendly panel-and-rail system as all other Everlock vinyl products. Panels slide into rails, and the installation is straightforward for most homeowners. Professional installation is also available if you prefer.

Does EverRock look as realistic as Novik stone? +

No. EverRock is textured vinyl, so it has more character than flat vinyl but less depth and realism than cast-polymer Novik panels. Novik products are designed to replicate actual stone or brick with considerable detail. If you need that level of realism, Novik is the better choice.

What warranty does EverRock carry? +

A Lifetime Warranty on materials and workmanship, same as every Everlock product. The warranty covers defects under normal use conditions and applies to the original purchaser for the life of the home.
