Novik Field Stone: Natural Stone Appearance, Organic and Irregular

Novik Field Stone skirting gives manufactured homes an authentic natural stone appearance by mimicking stones that look like they were gathered from a field or riverbed and set by hand. The panels feature larger stone shapes with significant size variation and natural-looking irregular gaps, creating a casual, organic appearance rather than a formal or uniform pattern.

Field Stone is the second most expensive product in the Novik line, priced at from $4,050 for a reference home. It appeals to homeowners on acreage, in wooded areas, or in rural communities who want their home to blend into a natural setting rather than stand out with a manicured or formal stone appearance.

Like all Novik panels, Field Stone is manufactured using a high-impact polymer molded from real stone surfaces, giving it realistic texture and color variation. The panels carry a Limited Lifetime Warranty and are backed by factory-direct support from Everlock Systems since 1981.

What Makes Novik Field Stone Distinct

The defining feature of Field Stone is its organic, hand-gathered appearance. Each panel has larger stone shapes with significant size and shape variation, creating irregular joints and gaps that make the skirting look like actual fieldstones that were set in place by hand over time.

This differs from the other Novik products: Hand-Laid Brick is formal and uniform, Stacked Stone is contemporary and tightly horizontal, Hand-Cut Stone is refined and architectural, and Dry Stacked Stone is ultra-contemporary and minimalist. Field Stone occupies its own aesthetic space, appealing to a different sensibility.

Field Stone works particularly well when you want the home to harmonize with a natural landscape. Homes on acreage, in wooded settings, or in rural communities find that Field Stone makes the manufactured home look like a natural part of the land rather than an addition to it. The irregular, organic pattern suggests permanence and timelessness.

Where Novik Field Stone Works Best

Rural properties and acreage. Homes on 2-plus acre lots in rural areas find that Field Stone creates visual harmony with the natural landscape. The organic, hand-gathered appearance looks at home in open country.

Wooded and forest settings. Homes surrounded by trees or in communities nestled in wooded areas benefit from Field Stone's natural aesthetic. The irregular stone pattern complements rather than contrasts with the natural surroundings.

Agricultural communities. Farming regions and agricultural areas see Field Stone as a fit with the rural character. The stone-gathered-from-the-land appearance resonates with homeowners who work the land.

Pacific Northwest and mountain regions. Areas with natural stone heritage, including the Pacific Northwest, Appalachian Mountains, and Southern mountain regions, have strong cultural affinity for Field Stone. Homeowners in these regions often prefer the hand-gathered aesthetic over uniform or formal stone patterns.

Homeowners seeking casual natural stone. Anyone who wants a natural stone appearance but prefers the organic, less formal look of field stone over the architectural refinement of Hand-Cut Stone or the uniformity of Hand-Laid Brick will find Field Stone the perfect fit.

Novik Field Stone and FHA and VA Loan Requirements

Novik composite skirting is viewed favorably by FHA and VA appraisers because the rigid, durable construction conveys long-term structural integrity. The stone appearance reinforces the perception of durability compared to lightweight vinyl.

Appraisers check for full perimeter enclosure, functional access door, durable material, and good overall condition. Novik products meet all these requirements. However, lender standards vary. Some lenders have specific product lists, and some conduct their own material assessment.

Installation of Novik Field Stone

Novik Field Stone panels are heavier and more rigid than vinyl, requiring professional installation in most cases. Panel weight and the need for secure bracing make this a job for experienced installers.

Professional recommended. Given the panel size, weight, and the importance of proper bracing to maximize wind resistance and durability, professional installation is recommended for Field Stone.

What installation includes: Site preparation, panel leveling, top and bottom rail installation, panel fastening, corner trim, and access door installation. Ventilation requirements depend on your local climate and site conditions.

Timeline: Professional installation typically takes one to three days depending on home size and site access. Turnaround for scheduling varies by region.

Novik Field Stone: Common Questions

Does Field Stone look fake up close? +

Novik panels are molded from real stone surfaces, so the texture, color variation, and detail are quite realistic. From a distance, Field Stone looks like actual field stone. Up close, experienced eyes can tell it is composite, but the appearance is convincing. We recommend ordering a sample so you can evaluate it yourself.

Will the earthy colors fade over time? +

The color in Novik Field Stone is molded into the panel during manufacturing, not painted onto the surface. This means color is integral to the material and will not fade or peel. The Limited Lifetime Warranty covers the panel material itself, so fade or discoloration would be covered under warranty.

Is Field Stone heavier than vinyl skirting? +

Yes. Novik Field Stone panels are heavier than vinyl, weighing approximately 3 to 4 lbs per linear foot. They are lighter than real stone but considerably heavier than vinyl. This weight provides superior bracing and wind resistance. If you are considering a DIY installation, factor the weight into your planning. Professional installation is recommended.

Does Field Stone work in wet or humid climates? +

Yes. The polymer material used in Novik Field Stone is moisture-resistant and will not rot, swell, or mildew. It performs well in wet, humid, and coastal environments. The Limited Lifetime Warranty covers the material in all climates under normal conditions.

How does Field Stone differ from Dry Stacked Stone? +

Field Stone is organic and irregular, designed to look like stones gathered from a field or river and set by hand. Dry Stacked Stone is contemporary and minimalist, with clean lines and minimal visible joints. They appeal to entirely different aesthetic sensibilities. Field Stone is best for rural, wooded, and traditional settings. Dry Stacked Stone is best for modern and contemporary homes. Dry Stacked Stone is also more expensive.
