What Sets Gorilla Insulated Apart

All four of our vinyl products provide wind resistance and durability. Gorilla is the heavy-duty option: heavier panel construction and higher insulation performance than Thermal EverRock.

Panel construction: Gorilla panels are thicker and denser than standard vinyl. The added weight provides superior wind resistance when properly anchored. Under sustained cold-air infiltration, the higher-density insulation core maintains R-value better than lighter insulation in Thermal EverRock.

Insulation performance: Gorilla is built with a higher-density foam insulation layer. Homes elevated on piers in cold climates with significant air movement underneath benefit most from this performance level. The difference matters on utility bills over ten years.

Built for demanding conditions: When climate is a serious factor and you want the most capable insulated vinyl product in the lineup, this is it. Gorilla assumes conditions that push lighter products to their limits.

Gorilla Insulated vs Thermal EverRock

Both products are insulated vinyl. Both provide excellent durability and cold-air exclusion. The key difference is performance under the most demanding conditions.

Thermal EverRock is the step up from standard vinyl and handles most cold-climate applications very well. It balances cost and performance. For homes in North Carolina, upstate New York, or Colorado at moderate elevations, Thermal EverRock often exceeds customer expectations.

Gorilla is the heavy-duty option for the most demanding climates. Heavier panel construction, higher-density insulation core. For severe climates with extended freezing periods and high wind loads, Gorilla is worth the difference.

The price difference is modest: Thermal EverRock from $2,974 versus Gorilla from $3,101 for a 14' x 66' home. For severe climates, the $127 difference on a typical home is a small premium for the performance upgrade.

Gorilla Insulated vs Novik Stone

These are fundamentally different product categories. Understanding the choice makes the decision clear.

Gorilla is the top insulated vinyl. It provides maximum insulation performance in a vinyl product category. No stone or brick appearance, but exceptional thermal performance and durability.

Novik (starting from $3,320 for Hand-Laid Brick) is cast polymer with much higher visual realism. Stone and brick appearance that mimics real masonry. Excellent weather protection and durability, but no insulation component. Novik is a decorative choice first, weather protection second.

The choice is not a simple comparison. It is between insulation performance (Gorilla) and visual premium appearance (Novik). If appearance and weather protection both matter to you, consider your priority. Cold climates favor Gorilla. Coastal and high-visibility communities often favor Novik for aesthetic reasons.

Installation and Professional Guidance

Professional installation strongly recommended. The panels are heavier than standard or lightly insulated vinyl. Proper level installation and secure anchoring are critical for long-term performance. Improper installation can compromise both insulation value and wind resistance.

Wind bracing: In high-wind areas, the top rail must be anchored securely to the home frame with bolts or lag screws. This is not optional.

Leveling: Uneven installation creates gaps and reduces insulation effectiveness. A professional installer will measure, shim, and secure panels for consistent performance.

What makes Gorilla Insulated different from Thermal EverRock? +

Both are insulated vinyl. Gorilla has heavier panel construction and higher-density foam insulation. For severe winter climates with extended freezing temperatures and high wind loads, Gorilla provides superior insulation performance. Thermal EverRock handles most cold-climate applications well and is the right choice for moderate cold-climate homes.

It is possible, but professional installation is strongly recommended. The panels are heavy, and proper level installation is critical for both insulation and wind resistance.
