Why Skirting Is a Critical Issue for Florida Manufactured Home Owners

Florida has more manufactured homes than almost any other state, and it faces environmental hazards that test skirting like nowhere else. Hurricanes. Flooding. Year-round humidity above 70%. Salt air within 30 miles of the coast. UV radiation that pounds down relentlessly 365 days a year. These forces combine to break down inferior skirting in just a few seasons.

Beyond climate, Florida manufactured home communities are heavily regulated. Many have strict HOA appearance standards that mandate matching skirting colors, complete perimeter coverage, and visible access doors. Because the state has a large retiree population and a deep veteran population seeking affordable housing, FHA and VA loan requirements are common statewide. Appraisers in Florida conduct skirting inspections like nowhere else, which means the right product choice impacts whether a home appraises at all.

This guide covers everything a Florida manufactured homeowner needs to know to choose skirting that holds up to hurricane-force winds, salt air, 95% humidity, and the toughest inspection standards in the country.

What Florida's Climate Does to Mobile Home Skirting

Hurricanes and High Winds

Florida has more hurricane landfalls than any other state. Most of the peninsula falls within FEMA Wind Zone II or III, with coastal areas designated as high-velocity hurricane zones. Skirting must withstand sustained winds of 100 to 130 mph without becoming a projectile hazard.

Thicker, heavier panels anchored securely to the home frame significantly outperform lightweight vinyl. Lightweight panels can separate from the frame under sustained wind load, creating gaps that allow flying debris to breach and cause structural damage.

Flooding and Elevated Homes

Many Florida manufactured homes in flood zones (AE or VE) are elevated on piers with 18 to 36 inches of clearance. Skirting must allow water to pass through during flooding without creating hydrostatic pressure that pushes panels inward and lifts the home off its foundation.

Some flood zone installations use breakaway panels designed to separate during water flow, then be replaced after the flood. This approach protects home structure while meeting FEMA requirements for elevated structures.

Humidity and Salt Air

Year-round humidity above 70% causes inferior vinyl to warp, yellow, and grow mildew. Salt air within 20 to 30 miles of any coastline accelerates surface degradation on unprotected materials. Everlock's UV-stabilized vinyl formulations are specifically designed to resist both moisture and salt air corrosion.

UV and Heat

Florida receives more solar radiation per square mile than any continental U.S. state. Vinyl that isn't UV-stabilized can fade in as little as 2 to 3 years. Color retention over a decade requires UV inhibitors built into the panel composition during manufacturing. Panel surfaces regularly exceed 140 degrees Fahrenheit, which softens lower-quality polymers and causes expansion, contraction, and warping.

HOA and Community Rules

Florida has thousands of manufactured home communities with specific appearance codes governing skirting color coordination, panel line alignment, and gap prevention. Violating appearance standards can result in fines or restrictions on home sales until corrected.

Florida Regional Skirting Guide: What Works Where

South Florida: Miami, Fort Lauderdale, the Keys

South Florida faces the highest wind zone classification in the state and the most aggressive salt air environment. Homes within 5 miles of the ocean experience year-round salt spray that degrades surface finishes. HOA appearance standards are among the strictest.

Recommended: Novik Faux Stone or EverRock. Novik's rigid composite construction offers the best wind resistance and holds up to salt air better than lighter vinyl. For budget-conscious buyers, EverRock handles both coastal environment and HOA requirements well.

Note: Florida Keys homes may have specific county code requirements for elevated structures. Always verify local codes before ordering.

Central Florida: Orlando, Tampa, Lakeland

Central Florida has the largest concentration of manufactured homes in the state. The region gets less direct coastal wind than South Florida but faces serious hurricane risk from storms tracking up from the Gulf. Extreme heat and humidity are constant.

Recommended: EverRock or Everlock Deluxe. EverRock's heavier construction is worth the modest premium for homeowners near Tampa Bay. Everlock Deluxe is excellent for inland areas where hurricane wind loads are lower.

North Florida and the Panhandle

North Florida is cooler, with Tallahassee averaging 20 to 25 nights below freezing per year. The region experiences high humidity and significant Gulf hurricane risk. Pensacola and Panama City have seen multiple major hurricane landfalls.

Recommended: Thermal EverRock for North Florida and the Panhandle. It handles both cold winter nights and hurricane-season wind loading. The insulation layer provides R-value that reduces heating costs. Gorilla Insulated for maximum insulation and impact resistance.

Florida Installation Requirements and Best Practices

Wind-load bracing: In FEMA Wind Zone II and III, the top rail should be anchored to the home frame with bolts or lag screws. This is not optional in high-wind zones.

Flood zone installations: Homes in VE zones must have breakaway panel installation or elevated skirting designed to allow water flow. Hydrostatic pressure can lift a home off foundation. Verify your zone classification with county building department.

Ventilation: Florida's humidity demands proper ventilation. Standard is one vent per 150 square feet. In high-humidity areas, consider one vent per 100 square feet.

Mildew prevention: Keep panel bottoms at least 2 to 3 inches above soil grade to prevent moisture wicking that causes mildew and wood rot.

DIY vs. professional: Everlock Deluxe and EverRock are DIY-friendly. Elevated homes and flood-zone installations benefit from professional installation to ensure proper wind bracing and water flow design.

Licensed contractor requirement: Some Florida counties require licensed mobile home installers for permitted work. Verify locally before installation.

FHA and VA Skirting Requirements for Florida Manufactured Homes

Florida has a large VA loan population. Both FHA and VA appraisers conduct thorough skirting inspections as part of home appraisal. An appraisal can fail if skirting doesn't meet lender requirements.

What appraisers check: Full perimeter enclosure, functional access door, durable material, and good overall condition with no visible rot or deterioration.

Novik and FHA/VA: Rigid composite panels are viewed more favorably by appraisers than lightweight vinyl. The durability perception in stone or brick appearance makes appraisers more confident in long-term structural integrity.

Common rejection items: Missing access door is the single most common rejection reason. Gaps between panels. Wood skirting (fire hazard). Visible cracks, holes, or damage.

Important disclaimer: Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. Always verify your lender's requirements in writing before ordering.

Florida Mobile Home Skirting: Common Questions

Is special skirting required for Florida hurricane zones? +

There is no single statewide Florida code that mandates a specific product. However, your local county, community, or lender may have requirements. In FEMA Wind Zone II and III areas, heavier-gauge panels anchored to the home frame will significantly outperform lightweight vinyl. EverRock and Novik are better choices in high-wind zones.

Does my skirting need to be hurricane-rated? +

Everlock products are not formally rated to a specific hurricane wind speed. However, our heavier products (EverRock, Novik) are designed to withstand significant wind loads when properly anchored. For engineered certifications required by specific counties, call 800-843-3336 for product specification sheets.

How does Florida's humidity affect vinyl skirting? +

High-quality UV-stabilized vinyl like Everlock's formulations holds up well to Florida humidity. Inferior vinyl can warp, yellow, and grow mildew within 2 to 3 years. Proper installation (bottom rail above grade, adequate ventilation) also reduces moisture problems significantly.

Do I need an access door in Florida? +

Yes. FHA and VA lenders require at least one access door. Most HOA rules and Florida community standards require the same. We sell access door kits that work with all our products. An access door is not optional.

Can I get skirting delivered to Florida quickly? +

Yes. Everlock Deluxe, EverRock, and Thermal EverRock typically ship within 3 business days and arrive within 7 to 10 business days. If you have a deadline, call 800-843-3336 and we'll tell you the exact timeline for your order.
