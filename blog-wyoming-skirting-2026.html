Mobile Home Skirting for Wyoming's Extreme Wind and Cold

Wyoming is the windiest state in the lower 48, and that fact shapes everything about skirting choice here. Cold is a factor, certainly. Temperatures routinely drop below minus 20 degrees Fahrenheit in winter. But wind is the defining challenge. Cheyenne regularly records the highest sustained wind speeds of any city in the country. Casper is not far behind. A manufactured home in Wyoming faces a combination of conditions that eliminates lightweight skirting options entirely.

Everlock has been supplying Wyoming homeowners since 1981. We know what holds up in Cheyenne, Laramie, Casper, and the high country. This guide is direct and practical. It focuses on panels that can handle sustained 50 to 60 mph gusts and frequent extreme-wind events without failing at the panel-rail connection.

Cold matters too. But in Wyoming, wind is the primary design constraint for skirting. Get the wind right, and the cold protection follows naturally from choosing heavier, more rigid panels.

Wyoming's Wind Reality, Measured

Cheyenne averages 12.9 mph wind year-round. That average hides the extremes. The city regularly sees gusts above 60 mph, and has recorded sustained winds in excess of 70 mph. Wyoming has some of the highest average wind speeds in the continental United States. Laramie, Sheridan, and areas around Casper all sit on high plains and mountain passes where wind acceleration is extreme.

What this means for skirting: panels with less mass flex more under wind pressure. Lightweight vinyl can rattle loose at fasteners. In extreme events, panels fail at the connection where they meet the rail. The solution is heavier panels locked into a secure rail system with proper bottom anchoring into firm ground.

Everlock and EverRock panels weigh more per square foot than basic vinyl. Gorilla Insulated panels are the heaviest in the standard lineup. That inertia matters. A heavier panel resists flex and movement under sustained wind load. When combined with proper bottom rail anchoring, the panel-rail connection stays tight even under sustained pressure.

Wind and Cold Together, the Compounding Effect

Wind chill matters more than raw air temperature for pipes underneath a manufactured home. A minus 10 degree Fahrenheit day with a 40 mph wind creates wind chill equivalent to minus 35 degrees Fahrenheit in still air. For the water lines and drain lines that run under a mobile home, minus 35 is the real number that matters.

Insulated skirting slows that heat loss. In Wyoming, where wind chill regularly exceeds minus 30 degrees, the insulation layer provides meaningful protection. Thermal EverRock Insulated has an R-value that reduces the rate of heat loss from beneath the home. Gorilla Insulated has even more insulation mass.

In Wyoming, wind chill makes insulation more important than the raw winter temperature suggests. An uninsulated vinyl panel stops at wind protection. An insulated panel does both.

Why Panel Weight Matters in Wind

Heavier panels have more inertia against wind flex. The Everlock rail system is engineered so panels cannot pull out under normal wind loads when properly installed. A lightweight panel may move within the rail system, creating gaps and allowing wind pressure to build underneath. A heavy panel resists that movement.

Gorilla Insulated weighs more per panel than EverRock Vinyl or Thermal EverRock. That extra weight is a feature in Wyoming. Less panel movement means less flex at fasteners. Less flex means fasteners stay tight longer.

Bottom rail anchoring into firm ground is the most critical installation step for wind performance anywhere, but it is essential in Wyoming. A bottom rail that is merely nailed into loose ground will allow lift-up and rattle under sustained wind. The same rail bolted or anchored into firm soil or concrete prevents that movement entirely.

Installation Best Practices for Wyoming Wind

Choose calm conditions: Wyoming has calm mornings even in high-wind locations. Schedule installation during early morning hours when wind is lowest. Avoid installation on high-wind days.

Anchor the bottom rail firmly: Drive bolts or anchors deep into firm soil. Do not rely on nails alone. In areas with frozen ground, anchor before deep freeze or wait for spring. Loose ground will not hold the bottom rail secure.

Have a second person: High-wind areas need two people to hold panels during installation. One person cannot manage both wind pressure and fastening without risk of dropped panels or loose installation.

Secure the top rail at close intervals: Space top rail fasteners no more than 16 inches apart. Make sure fasteners are firmly attached to the home frame, not just to skirting rail.

HUD Requirements and Wyoming Code

Wyoming manufactured homes must comply with HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. Skirting is required to be durable, allow water drainage, and provide ventilation. Everlock products meet these standards.

FHA and VA loans: If your home is financed with an FHA or VA loan, the appraisal process includes skirting inspection. Heavier, more durable skirting like Gorilla Insulated or EverRock passes appraisal more reliably than lightweight vinyl.

Wyoming Mobile Home Skirting: Common Questions

Will my skirting panels blow off in a Wyoming windstorm? +

Properly installed panels in the Everlock rail system are designed to stay locked under high wind. Installation quality, specifically bottom rail anchoring and top rail attachment, is the most important factor. A panel bolted at the bottom and fastened at close intervals to the top rail will not separate under sustained wind load. Loose installation or nails alone in soft ground will fail.

Is Gorilla Insulated worth the extra cost over Thermal EverRock in Wyoming? +

For Cheyenne, Laramie, Casper, or any exposed site, yes. The heavier Gorilla panel handles sustained wind better and resists flex-induced fatigue at fasteners. The insulation difference matters at Wyoming wind-chill temperatures. If your site is in a valley or more sheltered area, Thermal EverRock is a solid choice and provides good value.

Do you ship to rural Wyoming? +

Yes, we ship throughout the contiguous United States, including rural Wyoming. Call us at 800-843-3336 for remote delivery questions. We can discuss freight timing and options for your specific location.

Can one person install Everlock skirting? +

Technically yes, but two people make it much easier, especially in any wind. For Gorilla Insulated with its heavier panels, two people is strongly recommended. The second person can hold panels steady while you secure fasteners, which is safer and results in tighter installation.

What is the best time of year to install skirting in Wyoming? +

Fall before freeze is ideal. You have ground still soft enough to anchor properly, and cold weather does not affect installation. Avoid installation in high-wind conditions or when temperatures are below 20 degrees Fahrenheit. Panels can become more brittle in extreme cold, which affects both handling and fastening.
