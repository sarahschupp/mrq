What Affects Your Total Cost

1. Material Choice

Material is the biggest driver of price. Everlock Deluxe Vinyl is our most affordable option. Insulated products cost more because of the rigid foam core inside each panel. Novik stone-look products are priced at a premium because of their realistic multi-layer molding and finish.

2. Home Size and Perimeter

The longer and wider your home, the more skirting you need. A 28 x 72 double-wide has roughly 2.5 times the perimeter of a 14 x 66 single-wide, so you can expect prices to scale accordingly. The clearance height (distance from ground to the bottom of your home) also matters. Greater height means taller panels and more material.

3. Complete Kit vs. Individual Parts

Our Complete Kits bundle panels, top and bottom rails, corner trim, and hardware for your home size. Kits are the most efficient way to order because everything is calculated for you, arrives in one shipment, and reduces the chance of missing pieces. You can also order components individually if you are replacing sections or adding to existing skirting.

4. Shipping

Freight is not included in the prices above. Shipping cost depends on your distance from our plants in Alabama and Mississippi. For most continental U.S. customers, freight runs in the range of $150 to $450. Your exact shipping cost is calculated at checkout.

5. Installation

If you install yourself, your labor cost is zero. Most homeowners with basic tools can complete a single-wide in a weekend. If you prefer to hire a local installer, expect to pay roughly $300 to $800 depending on home size and your area. We can walk you through measuring and ordering, so you show up with the right materials.

What Factory-Direct Actually Means for Your Price

When you buy skirting from a home improvement store or a dealer, you pay their markup on top of the manufacturer's price. When you buy from SkirtingDirect, you are buying directly from Everlock Systems, the manufacturer. The price you see is what we charge, with no middleman added.

For a homeowner buying a full kit, the difference can be a few hundred dollars compared to buying the same materials through a retailer or installer who marks up the product. That is the reason we built SkirtingDirect.com as a factory-direct channel.

Choosing the Right Product for Your Budget

If you want the lowest price: Everlock Deluxe Vinyl

Solid, durable, and available in multiple colors. This is the product most of our customers order. It handles most U.S. climates well and carries our full Lifetime Warranty.

If you want better weather performance: EverRock Vinyl

Heavier gauge than Deluxe Vinyl. More resistant to wind, impact, and high-wind areas. Still straightforward to install. A meaningful step up in durability without jumping to the insulated category.

If you have cold winters or frozen pipe problems: Thermal EverRock or Gorilla Insulated

Both options include a rigid foam core that holds heat under your home. If you have had pipes freeze, or if you are in a climate with sustained below-freezing temperatures, insulated skirting typically pays for itself in avoided repair costs and energy savings over a few years.

If appearance is the priority: Novik line

The Novik products are the most realistic-looking skirting we carry. If you are trying to match a brick or stone aesthetic, or your HOA has appearance requirements, Novik gives you a result that reads as genuine masonry from the street. Five distinct styles are available.

FHA and VA Loan Considerations

If you are financing a manufactured home, your lender may require specific skirting to meet HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. All of our products are manufactured to those standards and are accepted by many FHA and VA lenders.

One thing lenders commonly require is an access door for utility and inspection access under the home. Our EZ Access Doors are sold separately and install with any of our skirting systems. Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. We recommend confirming requirements with your lender before ordering.
