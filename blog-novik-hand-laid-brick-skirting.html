What Makes Hand-Laid Brick Different

Standard vinyl skirting is smooth and uniform. Novik Hand-Laid Brick uses high-impact polymer panels molded with authentic brick detail. Each panel features individual brick profiles, deep mortar line recesses, and intentional surface irregularities that replicate the way real bricks are laid one-by-one with varying position and texture.

The result is a skirting that catches light differently than flat vinyl, creates shadow and depth, and genuinely looks like masonry from 10 feet away. Closer inspection still convinces most viewers that real brick work is present. Many homeowners report that visitors are surprised to learn the foundation is composite, not actual brick.

Hand-Laid Brick panels are heavier and more rigid than standard vinyl, which also means superior resistance to wind damage and deformation in extreme heat. The rigid construction makes panels difficult to damage during installation, and they hold their shape for decades without warping.

Where Novik Hand-Laid Brick Works Best

HOA Communities with Appearance Standards

Many manufactured home communities and HOA neighborhoods require skirting that complements the overall community aesthetic. Communities built around traditional architectural themes, neighborhoods where brick is common in the region, or communities with strict appearance codes often approve Hand-Laid Brick without question. The authentic brick appearance satisfies the toughest appearance committees.

Established Neighborhoods with Quality Expectations

Homes in established manufactured home communities where neighbors have invested in quality finishes benefit from Hand-Laid Brick skirting. It signals that the home has been well maintained and upgraded, which often translates to better resale perception in the neighborhood.

FHA and VA Appraisal Scenarios

Appraisers for FHA and VA loans conduct detailed skirting inspections. Rigid composite panels like Hand-Laid Brick are perceived as more durable and structurally sound than lightweight vinyl. Appraisers often view brick-appearance skirting as evidence of a well-maintained home, which can influence appraisal outcomes favorably.

Resale-Focused Homeowners

If you plan to sell within the next five to ten years, Hand-Laid Brick adds curb appeal that buyers immediately notice. The traditional brick appearance appeals to a broader demographic than vinyl and may justify a small price premium in certain markets, especially where brick homes are common.

FHA and VA Loan Compliance

Novik Hand-Laid Brick satisfies HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards appearance requirements. The rigid composite construction, full perimeter coverage, and integrated access door design meet lender expectations for durable, complete skirting.

What appraisers specifically check: full perimeter enclosure with no gaps, at least one functional access door, durable material with no visible cracks or rot, and overall condition consistent with the home's value and age.

Hand-Laid Brick checks all those boxes. The rigid panels do not crack or warp easily, the color does not fade within normal lending timelines, and the appearance conveys solidity and quality to appraisers.

Installation Overview

Novik Hand-Laid Brick installation is moderate in difficulty. The panels are heavier than standard vinyl, which means they require proper support and careful anchoring, particularly in high-wind areas. Most professional installers can complete Hand-Laid Brick installations without special equipment.

Many experienced DIYers successfully install Hand-Laid Brick skirting, particularly if the home is not in an elevated flood zone or high-wind area. If your home requires wind-load bracing (bolted anchors to the frame) or flood-zone compliance (breakaway panels), professional installation is recommended to ensure structural safety and code compliance.

A standard installation includes bottom rails, panels, top rail, corner pieces, and access door. Installation typically takes 2 to 5 days depending on home size and installer experience. Professional installation costs vary by region but typically range from $400 to $800 plus materials.

Novik Hand-Laid Brick: Frequently Asked Questions

Is Novik Hand-Laid Brick actually real brick? +

No. Novik Hand-Laid Brick is a high-impact polymer composite panel manufactured to replicate the appearance of real hand-laid brick. The look is authentic, but the material is engineered polymer, not masonry. This gives you the traditional appearance without the cost and maintenance burden of actual brick.

Does Hand-Laid Brick need to be painted? +

No. Hand-Laid Brick does not require painting, sealing, or staining. The brick color and appearance are molded into the polymer during manufacturing. No ongoing maintenance for finish is required. This is one of the major advantages over real brick, which requires periodic resealing and repointing.

Will my HOA approve Novik Hand-Laid Brick? +

Most HOA communities with appearance standards approve Hand-Laid Brick without issues. The traditional brick aesthetic aligns with community appearance goals in most neighborhoods. However, each HOA has unique rules. Review your CC&Rs or call your HOA board before ordering to confirm approval. We can provide product specifications if your HOA requests them.

Can I install Hand-Laid Brick myself? +

Hand-Laid Brick is heavier than vinyl, which makes DIY installation more physically demanding. Experienced DIYers who are comfortable with tools and working at heights can install it. Many homeowners hire a professional because the weight requires proper support and the installation timeline is longer than vinyl. For elevated or flood-zone homes, professional installation is strongly recommended.

How does Hand-Laid Brick compare to other Novik products? +

Novik offers five distinct stone and brick products: Hand-Laid Brick, Stacked Stone, Premium Hand-Cut Stone, Field Stone, and Dry Stacked Stone. Each has a different appearance, with Hand-Laid Brick being the most traditional. All are premium composite products with similar durability and Lifetime Warranty.
