Vinyl vs Novik: Which Skirting Option Fits Your Home and Budget

Both vinyl and Novik faux stone skirting are manufactured by Everlock Systems and sold factory-direct to homeowners. But they serve different needs, budgets, and home aesthetics. Vinyl skirting is lightweight, affordable, and easy to install. Novik faux stone and brick offer realistic appearance, superior durability, and stronger HOA and lender appeal. The core question is which one fits your budget, your home's appearance goals, and your local HOA or lender requirements.

This guide walks you through the key differences, pricing, durability, installation complexity, and which category is right for your situation.

When Vinyl Makes More Sense

Budget-Conscious Homeowners

Vinyl skirting starts at $750 for a standard reference home. This is the lowest-cost option for homeowners who need functional, durable skirting without premium appearance features. Total installed cost typically ranges from $1,000 to $1,500 including professional installation.

Straightforward Installations

Vinyl panels are lightweight and slide into top and bottom rails. A DIY homeowner with basic tools can complete installation in a weekend. No heavy lifting required. This makes vinyl ideal for anyone who wants to avoid contractor costs or has mobility concerns.

Cold Climates

If you live in a climate where heating costs matter, Everlock offers Thermal EverRock and Gorilla Insulated Skirting. These insulated vinyl options provide R-value that reduces heat loss through the foundation area. Northern climates benefit from this energy efficiency advantage that Novik does not offer.

Quick Turnaround Needed

Vinyl products ship within 3 business days and arrive within 7 to 10 days. If you have an HOA deadline, a closing deadline, or a seasonal window, vinyl moves fast. Novik typically takes slightly longer due to manufacturing complexity.

When Novik Makes More Sense

HOA Communities with Appearance Standards

Many manufactured home communities require skirting that matches the aesthetic of surrounding homes. HOA boards often prefer stone or brick finishes for curb appeal. Novik faux stone and brick panels satisfy these appearance standards better than solid-color vinyl. If your HOA has specific appearance requirements, check if Novik finishes are on the approved list.

High-Wind and Coastal Areas

Novik panels are thicker and heavier than vinyl. In FEMA high-wind zones or coastal areas with extreme weather, Novik's superior impact resistance and wind load capacity matter. Thicker composite panels are less likely to separate from the frame during sustained gusts. If you're in Florida, coastal Texas, Louisiana, or hurricane zones, Novik holds up better.

Homeowners Focused on Resale Value

Stone and brick appearance adds to curb appeal and perceived home value. If you plan to sell in the next 10 years, Novik skirting often justifies its premium cost through improved listing perception and appraiser confidence. Appraisers see Novik as more durable than vinyl, which can support a higher final appraised value.

FHA and VA Appraisals

While both vinyl and Novik can meet FHA and VA requirements, appraisers are often more confident in Novik's longevity. Stone and brick appearance signals durability to lenders. If your appraisal outcome is critical to your financing or refinance, Novik reduces appraiser concerns about skirting adequacy.

A Note on FHA and VA Loans

Both vinyl and Novik skirting can satisfy FHA and VA requirements. Lenders require full perimeter enclosure, durable material, functional access door, and no visible rot or deterioration. Both categories meet these standards.

However, appraiser perception matters. Novik's stone appearance and heavier construction give appraisers more confidence in long-term durability. Vinyl is fully acceptable, but some appraisers are more conservative about light-gauge vinyl longevity. The difference is perception, not actual compliance.

Always verify your lender's specific requirements in writing before ordering. Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems.

Vinyl vs Novik: Common Questions

Can I mix vinyl and Novik panels on the same home? +

Technically, you can, but it's not recommended for appearance consistency. If you want mixed materials, consult with your HOA and call 800-843-3336 to discuss layout and rail compatibility. Most homeowners choose one product for the entire perimeter.

Is vinyl or Novik easier to repair or replace later? +

Both are repairable. Damaged panels can be unbolted and replaced. Vinyl panels are lighter and easier to handle when replacing singles. Novik is heavier but equally durable. Both retain their materials after installation, so long-term availability is similar. We recommend keeping spare panels for future repairs.

Do vinyl and Novik require different access doors? +

No. The same access door hardware works with both vinyl and Novik panels. The door frame mounts to the home frame, and panels bolt in place around it. Both product lines accommodate standard access door kits.

What's the real lifespan difference between vinyl and Novik? +

Vinyl typically lasts 15-25+ years with proper installation and ventilation. Novik typically lasts 25-40+ years. The difference is due to composite material density and thickness. Both are field-tested across decades. Proper ventilation, drainage, and avoiding moisture contact at the bottom extend both products' life significantly.

Can I paint vinyl or Novik skirting? +

Vinyl can be painted with exterior-grade acrylic paint. Novik's textured surface is designed to simulate stone finishes; painting over it obscures the texture. We recommend order the finish color you want from the factory rather than painting after installation. Call 800-843-3336 if you have color or finish questions before ordering.
