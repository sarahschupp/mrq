Installation Guide

How to Install Mobile Home Skirting Yourself

Updated April 2026 • By Everlock Systems • Weekend project guide

Plenty of homeowners hire out the installation. But plenty more do it themselves over a weekend and save $300 to $800 in labor. If you are comfortable with basic tools, installing Everlock Deluxe Vinyl or EverRock skirting is a realistic weekend project. We have been watching customers do it successfully since 1981.

This guide covers single-wide and double-wide installations. The steps are the same; a double-wide just takes longer and benefits from having a helper for the second day.

Start with a Complete Kit. Our Complete Kits include panels, rails, corner trim, and hardware calculated for your home size. Nothing to figure out, nothing missing when you start. See Complete Kits.

Tools You Will Need

Nothing exotic. If you have done basic home repairs, you already own most of this.

Tape Measure 25 ft or longer for accurate perimeter measurement
Cordless Drill For attaching top and bottom rails
Circular Saw or Tin Snips For trimming panels to fit corners and ends
Level (4 ft) Critical for getting rails straight
Chalk Line Helps keep bottom rail level across long runs
Safety Glasses and Gloves Required when cutting vinyl
Ladder For attaching the top rail to the home frame
Screwdriver (flat and Phillips) For fine adjustments and vent installation

Hardware is included with our Complete Kits. You will need galvanized screws for the rails (typically 1.5 inch for the top rail into the home frame, and concrete anchors or landscape stakes for the bottom rail depending on your ground surface).

Step 1: Measure Before You Order

Getting the measurement right before you order prevents the most common frustration: running short or ending up with a large amount of leftover material.

You need two numbers:

  • Total perimeter: Measure all four sides of your home and add them together. Round up to the nearest foot.
  • Clearance height: Measure the distance from the ground to the bottom of the home frame. Do this at multiple points around the home and use the tallest measurement. This determines the panel height you need.
Measure at the highest point, not the lowest. Ground level often varies around a home. If you measure at a low spot and order panels for that height, you will run short at the high spots. Always use your tallest clearance measurement.

Once you have those two numbers, our team can help you confirm what you need, or use the calculator on our website. Call 800-843-3336 if you have any doubt before ordering. Getting this right saves everyone time.

Step-by-Step Installation

Estimated time for a standard single-wide: 6 to 10 hours. Double-wide with a helper: one full day. These assume flat to moderately uneven ground. Very uneven ground adds time.

1

Install the Bottom Rail

The bottom rail sits on or just below grade and holds the bottom edge of each panel. Start at one corner and work your way around the perimeter.

  • On concrete or gravel pads: anchor with concrete screws or masonry anchors
  • On dirt or grass: use landscape stakes driven through the rail holes
  • Use your chalk line to keep the rail level along each run
  • Overlap rail sections at joints for a clean connection
A level bottom rail is the most important step. Everything else builds off it.
2

Install the Top Rail

The top rail attaches to the bottom of your home frame and holds the top edge of each panel. This is where the ladder comes in.

  • Hold the rail against the home frame and mark screw locations
  • Use 1.5 inch galvanized screws into the frame (not the siding)
  • Check that top and bottom rails are plumb with each other at the corners
  • The gap between rail and home siding is normal and covered by the panel flange
3

Slide in the Panels

With both rails in place, panels slide into the channels from the top. Start at a corner and work toward the middle on each wall.

  • Tilt the panel at an angle to seat the top into the top rail channel first
  • Then drop the bottom into the bottom rail channel
  • Panels interlock along their edges as you add each one
  • Cut the last panel on each run to fit using a circular saw or tin snips
Tip: In hot weather, leave a small gap at the end of each run for expansion. Vinyl expands and contracts with temperature. About 1/4 inch per 10 feet of panel run is a good rule.
4

Install Corner Trim

Corner trim pieces cover the panel edges at each corner of the home. They snap or screw into place over the panel ends and give the installation a finished look.

  • Plumb the corner trim with a level before fastening
  • Trim to height if your corners are shorter than standard trim length
  • Match corner trim color to your panel color
5

Install Foundation Vents and Access Door

Proper ventilation under your home is required by HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. Access doors are required by many lenders.

  • Cut openings for vents at locations that promote cross-ventilation (opposite sides of the home)
  • One square foot of vent area per 150 square feet of floor area is the general HUD guideline
  • Install at least one access door on a side of the home that allows full crawl-space entry
  • Our EZ Access Doors fit the same panel opening as a standard panel cutout
FHA/VA loans: If your home is financed through an FHA or VA loan, your lender may have specific requirements for vent placement and access door size. Confirm with your lender before installation. Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems.
6

Backfill and Finish

Once all panels, vents, and the access door are in, backfill any gaps between the bottom rail and ground with soil, gravel, or landscape material to prevent air infiltration and pest entry.

  • Pack soil or gravel firmly against the outside of the bottom rail
  • Do not bury the bottom rail deeply; you want it accessible for future adjustments
  • Check the full perimeter and press any panels that are not fully seated
  • Walk the perimeter and look for gaps, loose panels, or trim that needs adjustment

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake What Happens How to Avoid It
Measuring at the low point of the grade Panels are too short at high points Always measure at the tallest clearance point
Skipping the chalk line on the bottom rail Wavy bottom rail, panels look uneven Snap a chalk line before setting the rail
Attaching top rail to siding instead of frame Rail pulls loose over time Find the structural frame behind the siding and screw into it
No expansion gaps on panel runs Panels buckle in summer heat Leave 1/4 inch per 10 feet at the end of each run
Installing vents only on one side Poor air circulation, moisture problems Install vents on opposite sides for cross-ventilation
Forgetting the access door Fails lender or inspection requirement Install at minimum one access door per HUD standards

Common Installation Questions

Do I need a building permit?

Requirements vary by state, county, and manufactured home community. Some counties require a permit for skirting as part of a home improvement project. Others do not. Check with your local building department or community management before you start.

What if my ground is very uneven?

Uneven ground is common. Most installations handle 2 to 4 inches of grade variation without major issues by adjusting the bottom rail height at low spots or trimming panel bottoms at high spots. Extreme grade changes (more than 6 to 8 inches) may require grading work before skirting installation. Call us and describe your situation; we can advise you on the best approach.

Can I install skirting in winter?

Yes, with care. Vinyl is less flexible in cold temperatures and more prone to cracking if forced. Work in the warmest part of the day and handle panels carefully. Do not strike panels with tools when temperatures are below freezing.

My panels are slightly too tall. Do I cut from the top or the bottom?

Cut from the bottom. The top edge seats into the top rail channel and needs to remain clean and unmodified. Trim the bottom edge with a circular saw set to a shallow depth or with tin snips for small amounts.

What if I damage a panel during installation?

Call us. If a panel is defective from the factory, the Lifetime Warranty applies. If it was damaged during installation, we can usually help you order a replacement panel at standard pricing. Panels are sold individually, not just in full kits.
