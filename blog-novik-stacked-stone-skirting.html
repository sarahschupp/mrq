Introducing Novik Stacked Stone

Novik Stacked Stone skirting delivers a clean, modern aesthetic that transforms the foundation of any manufactured home. Unlike traditional faux brick or irregular stone patterns, Stacked Stone features a horizontal coursing design that reads as contemporary and sophisticated. Each panel is molded with realistic stone texture and shadow lines, creating depth and visual interest without the maintenance burden of real stone.

Manufactured by Everlock Systems at our U.S. plants, Novik Stacked Stone is engineered for durability in challenging climates. The horizontal profile naturally sheds water, the high-impact polymer resists UV fading, and the rigid composite construction anchors securely to manufactured home frames. It's an ideal choice for homeowners who want authentic stone appearance without excessive cost or installation complexity.

What Makes Stacked Stone Stand Out

Contemporary Design

Stacked Stone's horizontal courses create a modern, architectural aesthetic that pairs beautifully with contemporary home exteriors. The clean horizontal lines suggest movement and elegance, making the foundation of a manufactured home a design feature rather than an afterthought.

Realistic Texture

Each panel is molded with natural-looking stone texture and shadow lines that catch light realistically. From a distance, Stacked Stone is visually indistinguishable from genuine stacked fieldstone. Close inspection reveals the engineered composition, but the visual impact remains substantial.

Water Shedding

The horizontal stacked profile is inherently efficient at shedding water. Water flows down the slope of each course rather than pooling or running into seams. This design detail reduces moisture exposure around the foundation and minimizes the conditions that encourage mildew or deterioration.

Low Maintenance

Unlike real stone, Stacked Stone requires no sealing, repointing, or specialized cleaning. An occasional rinse with a garden hose restores appearance. The durable polymer finish resists staining and UV fading for decades.

Moderate Installation Difficulty

Stacked Stone panels are heavier than vinyl, which means careful fastening and anchoring, but installation follows straightforward panel-and-rail assembly methods. Not a DIY product for inexperienced installers, but manageable for professionals or experienced homeowners comfortable with power tools.

Where Stacked Stone Works Best

Mountain Communities and Outdoor-Oriented Regions

Mountain communities value homes that blend with natural surroundings. Stacked Stone's organic aesthetic feels at home in Colorado mountain towns, Pacific Northwest communities, Utah ski resort areas, and similar regions where stone architecture is expected.

Contemporary Subdivisions and HOA Communities

Newer subdivisions and HOA communities often feature contemporary architectural styles. Stacked Stone's horizontal lines align with modern design sensibilities and satisfy appearance requirements in communities that prohibit traditional brick or irregular stone patterns.

Pacific Northwest and Moisture-Heavy Climates

The water-shedding profile and UV-stabilized polymer make Stacked Stone ideal for high-rainfall regions. The horizontal coursing drains moisture efficiently, reducing the conditions that encourage moss, mildew, or wood rot on adjacent structures.

Properties Where Stone Appearance Is Desired But Budget Is Limited

Real stone skirting or hand-laid brick simulation products can exceed $5,000. Stacked Stone offers authentic stone presence at a moderate price point, making it accessible to homeowners on realistic budgets.

Homes with Diverse Exterior Finishes

Stacked Stone pairs well with fiber cement siding, metal roofing, composite shingles, and contemporary cladding materials. Its neutral stone appearance doesn't clash with varied exterior colors and textures.

Installation Overview: Novik Stacked Stone

Weight and support: Stacked Stone panels are heavier than vinyl and require rigid aluminum or steel rails for support. The home frame must be able to safely bear the panel weight when fully installed.

Fastening: Panels bolt or screw to the rail system with stainless steel hardware to resist corrosion. Proper fastening and spacing prevent wind-load failures and weather intrusion.

Seaming: Panel-to-panel seams are sealed with exterior-grade silicone to prevent water ingress. Quality seaming is critical to long-term performance.

Access door: At least one functional access door is required for foundation ventilation, utility access, and FHA/VA compliance. Novik access doors are sized to fit the panel profile.

Professional installation recommended: While Stacked Stone is not beyond reach of experienced DIY installers, professional installation ensures proper weight distribution, secure anchoring, and weathertight sealing. Many homeowners choose professional installation for peace of mind.

Novik Stacked Stone: Common Questions

Is Novik Stacked Stone real stone? +

No. Novik Stacked Stone is a high-impact polymer composite panel with stone texture and shadow lines molded into the surface. The realistic appearance is engineered; the material is durable polymer. This composition delivers the aesthetic of stone without real stone's weight, fragility, and maintenance demands.

How does Stacked Stone hold up in cold climates? +

Novik Stacked Stone is engineered to withstand temperature extremes. The polymer composite resists brittle cracking in freezing temperatures and softening in high heat. The horizontal stacked profile actively sheds water and ice melt, reducing freeze-thaw stress. Stacked Stone performs reliably in cold climates, mountain regions, and northern states.

Can I mix Stacked Stone with other skirting products? +

Mixing different skirting products on the same home is generally not recommended. Visual consistency around the entire perimeter creates a cohesive appearance and satisfies HOA rules. Stick with one product family for best results. If your home has specific zoning or elevation changes, call 800-843-3336 to discuss mixed-product strategies.

Will Stacked Stone satisfy my HOA requirements? +

Most HOA communities accept Novik products because they present as genuine material, are maintenance-free, and are specifically manufactured for manufactured homes. However, not every HOA has identical standards. Before ordering, review your CC&Rs (covenants, conditions, and restrictions) or contact your HOA board to confirm that composite stone skirting is approved. If your community has photo guidelines or color examples, verify your Stacked Stone color matches community preferences.

How does Stacked Stone differ from Field Stone or Hand-Cut Stone? +

Stacked Stone features clean, horizontal courses creating a modern, architectural look. Field Stone has irregular, randomly-laid courses mimicking natural fieldstone, creating a rustic, organic character. Premium Hand-Cut Stone features precise geometric blocks with sharp edges, creating a high-end, refined appearance. Stacked Stone is the middle option: more contemporary than Field Stone, less formal than Hand-Cut Stone, and priced accordingly.
