Why Skirting Matters More in Texas Than Almost Anywhere Else

Texas is one of the most demanding environments for manufactured home skirting in the country. The state spans five distinct climate zones, from subtropical Gulf Coast to the high desert of West Texas to the cold plains of the Panhandle. Homeowners face extreme UV exposure, summer heat that pushes 110 degrees, hail storms that can strip panels in minutes, and Gulf-driven hurricanes that test every fastener in the system.

Choosing the right skirting for your Texas home means choosing a product engineered for your specific region's weather, soil conditions, and code requirements. This guide walks you through every major skirting option available in 2026, with regional recommendations so you get the right product the first time.

Texas Climate and Code Challenges: What Skirting Has to Survive

Texas manufactured home skirting must survive conditions that would fail lesser products. Understanding these challenges is the first step to choosing skirting that will last.

Heat and UV Degradation

West Texas and South Texas routinely see temperatures between 100 and 110 degrees during summer months. Standard-grade vinyl, if not UV-stabilized, can fade, crack, and become brittle in as little as two to three years under this intense sun exposure. Premium vinyl formulations with UV inhibitors significantly extend the lifespan of skirting panels and maintain color consistency year after year.

Wind and Storm Exposure

The DFW metroplex sits in one of the most active tornado and derecho corridors in North America. South Texas and the Gulf Coast experience hurricane-force winds rated to 130+ mph. Proper skirting installation includes wind-rated fastening systems that secure the top rail to the home frame, especially in FEMA Wind Zones II and III. Heavier-gauge panels and rigid composite materials like Novik hold up better to sustained high-wind exposure than thinner vinyl options.

Hail Damage

Texas leads the United States in hail damage claims. The DFW and Hill Country regions see multiple severe hail events per year that can produce stones the size of baseballs. Skirting panels need impact-resistant construction. Thicker, heavier vinyl panels and rigid composite panels absorb hail impact better than thin standard vinyl. No skirting is completely hailproof in an extreme storm, but material choice significantly reduces the likelihood of visible damage.

Soil Movement and Foundation Issues

Much of Texas sits on expansive clay soils that swell with moisture and shrink during droughts. This soil movement causes foundation movement, which in turn stresses rigid skirting systems. Flexible vinyl panels handle minor foundation movement better than masonry-look composite panels. Newer homes on proper piers with adequate drainage typically experience minimal soil-related skirting problems.

HOA and Community Appearance Rules

Texas manufactured home communities have some of the strictest appearance codes in the country. Many parks require matched, maintained skirting and prohibit wood or fabric enclosures. HOA violations can result in fines and mandatory replacement. Masonry-look panels like Novik often satisfy HOA requirements more easily than plain vinyl because they signal permanence and quality.

FHA and VA Loan Requirements

Texas has a large VA loan population for manufactured homes. Appraisers actively inspect skirting condition and compliance with HUD standards as part of the approval process. Missing access doors, gaps in panels, and deteriorated or wood skirting are common rejection reasons. Well-installed, maintained skirting made from approved materials significantly improves appraisal outcomes.

Texas Regional Skirting Guide: Which Product for Which Part of the State

Texas spans multiple climate zones and building code jurisdictions. The best skirting choice for Houston differs significantly from the best choice for Amarillo or El Paso. This section breaks down regional conditions and recommended products for each major part of the state.

Gulf Coast and Houston Area

The Texas Gulf Coast experiences sustained wind speeds of 100+ mph during hurricane season and year-round high humidity that stresses vinyl panels through thermal cycling. Hurricane season (June through November) brings multiple tropical systems that test every fastener and seam in the skirting system.

For Gulf Coast homes, we recommend EverRock Vinyl or Novik panels. EverRock's heavier gauge (typically 0.055 to 0.065 inches) handles coastal winds better than standard vinyl. Novik's rigid composite construction offers the strongest wind resistance of any product on the market. Proper anchoring to the home frame is equally important as product choice in high-wind zones. A representative customer experience from Pasadena, TX involved a homeowner who replaced standard vinyl after Hurricane Harvey and switched to EverRock. It survived two additional hurricane seasons with no panel damage or fastener failure.

North Texas and Dallas-Fort Worth

The DFW metroplex sits in a severe storm corridor with active tornado risk, damaging hail, and wind shear events that can occur any time of year. The region experienced over 2,500 hail events in the past decade, making impact-resistant skirting a practical necessity rather than an optional upgrade.

For DFW homes, we recommend Thermal EverRock for homeowners concerned about hail impact. The thermal panels feature heavier construction and foam backing that adds impact absorption, making them more resilient to hail damage than standard vinyl. EverRock standard-grade is also a solid choice for budget-conscious DFW homeowners. Note that tornado-rated anchoring systems are a separate product category from skirting. Skirting provides aesthetic enclosure and insulation, not structural bracing.

Central Texas and Austin/San Antonio

Central Texas manufactured home communities have experienced explosive growth, with active HOA enforcement of appearance standards. Austin and San Antonio both have strict community rules around skirting condition and uniformity. Additionally, much of Central Texas sits on expansive clay soils that cause foundation movement during wet-dry cycles.

For Central Texas, we recommend Everlock Deluxe Vinyl for budget-conscious installs that still meet HOA requirements, or Novik panels if HOA rules are strict or if the community has specific masonry-look preferences. Flexible vinyl panels handle minor foundation movement better than rigid masonry panels. If you are in a high-heat area with significant sun exposure, EverRock is an excellent middle-ground option offering premium UV stability at reasonable cost.

West Texas (Midland, Odessa, El Paso)

West Texas experiences extreme heat and intense UV exposure. Midland and Odessa average summer temperatures of 100+ degrees with very low humidity, creating conditions that cause inferior vinyl to fade, crack, and embrittle in just 2 to 3 years. El Paso offers slightly lower temperatures but equally intense UV exposure and dust storms.

For West Texas, we recommend Everlock Deluxe or EverRock vinyl. Both use UV-stabilized formulations engineered to resist fading and surface degradation even in extreme heat. Lighter colors reflect heat better and tend to retain appearance longer than dark colors. Homeowners in West Texas often report that cheap, non-stabilized vinyl can become unusable after a single hot summer, making premium formulations a wise investment.

Texas Panhandle (Amarillo and Beyond)

The Texas Panhandle experiences cold winters with frequent freeze-thaw cycles, ice storms, and high winds from the plains. Amarillo averages 20 days below freezing per year. Pipe freeze-ups during hard freezes are a significant maintenance issue for many homeowners. Skirting plays an underappreciated role in protecting crawl space pipes from extreme cold.

For the Panhandle, we recommend Thermal EverRock or Gorilla Insulated Skirting. These are the only products that meaningfully reduce heat loss from the crawl space and provide measurable protection for water pipes during hard freezes. The R-value of insulated skirting (typically R-3 to R-5) combined with adequate ventilation can mean the difference between a frozen pipe and a functioning system on a 5-degree morning. Pipe protection alone justifies the upgrade cost in one winter for many Panhandle homeowners.

Texas-Specific Installation Tips

Proper installation is as important as product choice. Texas soil conditions, wind exposure, and heat all affect how skirting should be installed.

Foundation Types and Pier Requirements

Pier-and-beam is the most common foundation type in Texas. The skirting enclosure sits on a concrete or wood perimeter between the piers. Before installing skirting, verify that all piers are plumb and that the top of the frame is relatively level. Uneven ground clearance across a 70-foot-long home is normal, but severe variations (more than 6 inches) should be addressed before skirting installation.

Wind-Zone Bracing in Coastal and High-Wind Areas

In FEMA Wind Zone II and III areas (most of the Gulf Coast and parts of South and West Texas), the top rail of the skirting should be secured to the home frame with galvanized fasteners spaced at code-specified intervals (typically 16 to 24 inches). This prevents wind from lifting the entire skirting system during high-wind events. Many installation guides recommend stainless steel fasteners in coastal areas where salt air accelerates corrosion.

Ventilation for Texas Heat

Texas homes need adequate crawl space ventilation to prevent heat and moisture buildup under the home. HUD standards typically require one vent per 150 square feet of crawl space area. In high-humidity coastal areas, consider increasing vent density to one per 100 to 125 square feet. Inadequate ventilation can trap heat and moisture, potentially damaging insulation and wooden frame components.

DIY Feasibility

Everlock Deluxe and EverRock vinyl skirting are both designed for DIY installation. Both products use simple interlocking systems and require only basic hand tools (drill, level, tape measure, scissors). Most homeowners with basic carpentry skills can complete a single-wide installation in one weekend. Double-wides typically take two weekends. Novik panels require more precision at corners and transitions, making professional installation recommended for first-time installers.

When to Hire a Professional

Consider hiring a local installer if your home is in a flood zone, sits on a steep grade, or needs foundation leveling before skirting installation. Professional installers can often complete the work in one to two days and include a labor warranty on workmanship.

FHA and VA Skirting Requirements for Texas Manufactured Homes

Texas has a significant population of VA and FHA loans on manufactured homes. Both loan programs include skirting requirements that appraisers actively inspect during the appraisal process. Understanding these requirements helps you pass appraisal on the first try.

What Appraisers Look For

FHA and VA appraisers evaluate skirting for three key factors: complete perimeter enclosure with no gaps, an accessible vent or access door for crawl space inspection, and durable, maintained material appropriate for the home's location. Metal skirting is typically approved. Vinyl skirting must be in good condition (no missing panels, cracks, or evidence of water intrusion). Wood or fabric skirting is typically disapproved.

How Masonry-Look Skirting Helps Approvals

Novik faux brick and stone panels frequently receive positive comments from appraisers. Appraisers often note that masonry-look skirting signals permanence and long-term investment in the home, which can influence appraised value positively. This is not a guarantee, but the aesthetic signal can help when an appraiser has discretion on borderline valuation decisions.

Common Rejection Reasons

The most frequent skirting-related rejection reasons include missing or non-functional access door or vent, gaps between panels or gaps at ground level, deteriorated wood skirting or fabric enclosures, and visible evidence of water damage or mold on existing skirting. New skirting installation nearly always satisfies these concerns.

FHA/VA Compliance Disclaimer

Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. Requirements can vary by lender and loan program. Always check with your lender before ordering skirting for an FHA or VA financed home.

Frequently Asked Questions: Skirting for Texas Mobile Homes

Does Texas have specific skirting codes for manufactured homes? +

Texas follows HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards (HUD Code) rather than state-specific skirting codes. Local municipalities and manufactured home communities may have additional appearance requirements. If you are in a flood zone, FEMA elevation requirements may also affect how your skirting is installed. Always check with your local building department and community rules before ordering.

What skirting survives Texas hail best? +

Thicker, heavier panels handle hail impact better than thin vinyl. EverRock and Thermal EverRock are heavier gauge than standard vinyl and hold up well in hail-prone areas like DFW and the Hill Country. No skirting is completely hailproof in a severe storm, but proper material selection significantly reduces the likelihood of damage. Novik composite panels offer the best impact resistance of all options.

Can I install skirting myself in Texas? +

Yes, for most home configurations. Everlock Deluxe and EverRock are both designed for DIY installation and come with complete instructions. A single-wide can typically be completed in a weekend by a homeowner with basic carpentry skills. Double-wides or homes with unusual ground clearance may be easier with a helper or a local installer.

Will Everlock skirting fade in the Texas sun? +

Everlock products use UV-stabilized formulations specifically to resist fading and surface degradation from sun exposure. Lighter colors reflect heat better and tend to look better longer in extreme heat climates. Some gradual color change over many years is normal with any vinyl product and is not covered under warranty.
