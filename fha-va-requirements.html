Skirting Requirements for FHA and VA Loans on Manufactured Homes

If your lender requires skirting as a condition of loan approval: here's what you need to know, and what to order.

Important: This page provides general guidance based on HUD and VA guidelines for manufactured housing. Final compliance determination rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. Requirements can vary by lender, loan program, and local jurisdiction.

Why Lenders Require Skirting

Lenders who issue FHA or VA loans on manufactured homes typically require the home to meet HUD Minimum Property Standards (MPS). Skirting is one of the items appraisers check because it protects the home's foundation, plumbing, and structural components from weather, pests, and moisture. Without acceptable skirting, a loan can be delayed or denied.

Protects the Structure

Skirting prevents moisture, pests, and extreme temperatures from damaging the underside of the home and its plumbing.

Required for HUD Compliance

HUD Minimum Property Standards require manufactured homes to have enclosure of the crawl space with a durable, perimeter skirting material.

Affects Appraisal Value

Appraisers evaluate the condition and completeness of skirting as part of the home's overall value assessment.

What Appraisers Typically Require

Compliance Checklist

✓
Full perimeter enclosure (no gaps, openings, or missing panels)
✓
Durable, weather-resistant material (vinyl, metal, concrete, or masonry)
✓
At least one access door or removable panel for utility access
✓
Adequate ventilation (one square foot of vent area per 150 square feet of crawl space)
✓
Panels in good condition (no major cracks, holes, warping, or rot)
✓
Proper ground clearance maintained

Access Door: Required for FHA/VA Compliance

An access door is not optional for FHA/VA loan approval. HUD requires at least one access panel or door in your skirting to allow inspection and maintenance of the area under the home. If your current skirting does not have one, or if you are installing new skirting, you need to add an access door kit.

What to Look For in an Access Door

✓
At least 18"x24" opening
✓
Weather-tight closure
✓
Matches skirting material or is compatible
✓
Opens and closes easily
