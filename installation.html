How to Install Mobile Home Skirting

A complete walkthrough from measuring your home to finishing the last panel. Most homeowners complete a single-wide in a weekend with basic tools.

1-2 days for most homes Basic tools required No special skills needed

1
Measure Your Home
Get accurate numbers before you order anything

Accurate measurements are the most important part of the whole project. Ordering too little means delays waiting for more material. Ordering too much wastes money. Take your time here and measure twice.

What to Measure

Home Length
Measure the full length of each long side, from corner to corner at ground level. Record both sides separately, they may differ slightly.
Home Width
Measure each short end of the home from corner to corner. For double-wides, note the location of any seam in the middle.
Height (Above Grade)
Measure from the ground to the bottom of the home frame at each corner. Heights often vary 2-4 inches around the perimeter. Use the tallest measurement for ordering.
Doors and Openings
Note the width of any existing access doors, utility openings, or crawl space vents. These reduce the total panel footage needed and factor into vent placement.
Pro Tip

Measure the perimeter in linear feet, then multiply height (in inches) by linear footage to get total square footage if using the calculator. Or just call us at 800-843-3336 and read your measurements to our team. We'll tell you exactly what to order.

For standard homes: a 14' x 66' single-wide typically needs around 160 linear feet of skirting panels. A 28' x 60' double-wide needs roughly 180 linear feet. Heights vary, so always measure your actual home.

2
Order Your Materials
Use our calculator or call for a custom parts list

Everlock complete kits include everything you need: panels, top and bottom rails, corner pieces, hardware, and installation instructions. You do not need to source components separately.

Choose your product based on your climate, budget, and aesthetic. Here is a quick summary of what each product requires at installation:

△
Everlock Deluxe Vinyl and EverRock Vinyl
Standard installation. Rails anchor to the ground and the home frame. Panels snap into rails with no fasteners required in the panel field. Easiest for first-time installers.
✥
Thermal EverRock Insulated and Gorilla Insulated Skirting
Same rail system as standard vinyl. The insulated core adds R-value and weight. Panels are slightly heavier but install the same way. Ideal for cold climates where pipe protection matters.
■
Novik Brick and Stone Panel Systems
These panels require a slightly different approach. They attach to a furring strip or Z-channel system along the home frame and are interlocked horizontally. Slightly more labor-intensive but achieves a finished masonry look. Call us before ordering to confirm your installation method.

Our online calculator will generate a material estimate for your home. For a precise parts list with quantities, call 800-843-3336. Our team in Boulder will walk through your measurements and tell you exactly what you need, including vents and access doors.

3
Gather Your Tools
Most of this is already in a typical garage

You do not need specialized tools to install Everlock skirting. Everything listed below is available at any hardware store if you do not already own it.

Tools Needed

Tape measure (25 ft. or longer)
Chalk line or string line
Circular saw or tin snips (for panel cuts)
Hammer or rubber mallet
Drill and bits
Level (4-ft. preferred)
Utility knife
Galvanized staples or screws (for top rail)
Safety glasses
Work gloves
Knee pads (optional but helpful)
Wheelbarrow or bucket (for leveling gravel)
On Cutting Vinyl Panels

A standard circular saw with a fine-tooth blade cuts vinyl cleanly. Cut on the finished face to reduce chipping. Tin snips work for small trims. A score-and-snap method also works for straight cuts on vinyl panels.

4
Prepare the Site
A level, clear perimeter makes everything easier

Before any panels go up, take time to prepare the ground along the perimeter. This step is quick but it directly affects how the finished job looks.

  • Clear vegetation, rocks, and debris from the full perimeter. You need clear access along the ground line.
  • Grade the soil so it slopes slightly away from the home for drainage. Standing water behind skirting accelerates corrosion and pest intrusion.
  • If the ground is soft or uneven, add a bed of compacted gravel under the bottom rail line. This gives a stable, level base.
  • Mark the bottom rail line with chalk or string. Use your level to ensure it is consistent around the full perimeter before you anchor anything.
  • Note utility penetrations and plan vent placement. HUD guidelines require foundation vents every 150 square feet of enclosed floor area. Everlock Foundation Vents fit the same rail system as your panels.
Before You Dig

If you plan to stake the bottom rail into the ground, call 811 (the national dig-safe number) to have underground utilities marked. This is required by law in most states and takes one business day.

5
Install the Panels
Start at a corner and work around the perimeter

Your kit comes with a full installation instruction sheet. What follows is an overview of the process for standard vinyl panel systems.

  • Attach the top rail to the bottom of the home frame using galvanized screws or staples. Run it level around the full perimeter. This rail holds the top of every panel.
  • Set the bottom rail along your chalk line. Anchor it to the ground with stakes included in the kit, or attach to a concrete footer if your site has one. Keep it level and flush with the top rail.
  • Install corner pieces first. Outside and inside corner sections lock both rails together at each corner and give you a clean starting reference for panel runs.
  • Slide panels into the rails one at a time, starting from a corner. Panels interlock vertically with a tongue-and-groove edge. No fasteners needed in the panel field for standard vinyl.
  • Cut the last panel in each run to fit. Measure carefully, add about 1/4-inch to the panel depth so it locks into the rail securely, and cut with a circular saw or snips.
  • Install vents and access doors as you go. Everlock Foundation Vents replace a standard panel in the run. EZ Access Doors replace a section of panel and rail to create a hinged opening.
Double-Wides

On a double-wide, treat each side independently and work toward the center seam. The marriage wall section between the two halves is typically handled with a continuous panel run. Call us if you have questions about your specific home configuration.

Most single-wides take 6-8 hours for the panel installation phase once the rails are set. Double-wides typically take a full day.

6
Finish and Inspect
A few final checks before you call it done

Once all panels are in, walk the full perimeter and check each of these before wrapping up:

  • All panels are fully seated in both top and bottom rails. Press firmly at each panel to confirm there is no play.
  • Corner pieces are flush and locked on both sides. No gaps at corners where pests or moisture can enter.
  • Foundation vents are open (or operable, if using closeable vents). Blocked vents cause moisture buildup under the home.
  • Access doors open and close freely. The hinge and latch should operate without binding.
  • No panel edges are visible above the top rail line. The top rail should conceal all panel edges cleanly.
  • Bottom rail is in contact with the ground along the full run. Any gaps larger than 1/2 inch should be filled with gravel or soil to prevent pest entry.
FHA and VA Loans

If your skirting installation is required for loan approval, keep a copy of your invoice and product specs. Your lender's appraiser will inspect that skirting is present, continuous, and in good condition. Everlock products meet HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. Final approval rests with your lender and appraiser, not with Everlock Systems. See our FHA/VA guide for details.

?
DIY or Hire a Pro?
Most people can do this themselves. Here is how to decide.

Everlock skirting is designed to be homeowner-installed. The rail-and-panel system does not require carpentry skills or special equipment. That said, there are situations where hiring a local installer makes sense.

Install It Yourself If...
  • You are comfortable with basic measuring and cutting
  • Your home has a standard rectangular footprint
  • The ground perimeter is relatively level
  • You have a free weekend and a helper
  • You want to save on labor costs
Consider a Pro If...
  • Your home has irregular perimeter angles or additions
  • Significant grading or ground prep is needed
  • Your lender requires a licensed contractor for loan approval
  • You are working with Novik stone panels (more complex)
  • Physical mobility makes ground-level work difficult

We do not install skirting ourselves. To find an installer, search for "mobile home installer" or "mobile home services" in your area. You can also call us at 800-843-3336 and we will try to help point you in the right direction, though we cannot guarantee a referral in every location.
